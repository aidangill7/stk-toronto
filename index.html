Chef's Selection

Slowing GDP Growth & Inflation in Canada

There are several current events across Canada right now that are affecting STK Toronto. Tonight, we will be focusing on the slowing GDP Growth in Canada, Inflation in Canada, alongside unemployment in Toronto. These are a personal favourite of Abid Chaudry, stating that these are the most influential factors affecting STK Toronto today.

In Canada recently, the GDP growth per year has been extremely lower than necessary for sufficient economic growth, with most years showing GDP growth near none. Abid Chaudry also stated that the elasticity of the steakhouse is heavily dependent on the performance of the economy; this is more heavily covered later on the menu. Getting into specific statistics, the GDP growth in Canada was 1.25% in 2023, 2.05% in 2024, and 1.74% in 2025. These numbers may give the illusion that Canada's economy is progressing and growing just at a slow rate. However, this is not true at all, as you have to look at external factors, like inflation. Inflation in Canada for 2023 was 3.88%, in 2024 it was 2.38%, and in 2025 it was 2.1%. This is evidence that in recent years, inflation is outstripping GDP growth, decreasing the purchasing power of individuals who reside in Canada and consumers of STK Steakhouse.

Since STK Toronto carries luxury goods, the income elasticity coefficient for the steakhouse is very high, meaning that when consumer income decreases from economic downturns, the demand for the steakhouse significantly decreases. Residents of Canada are not the only individuals feeling this economic recession, but so are businesses. STK Toronto's revenue has noticed a significant drop, with it previously generating about $11 million per year compared to only about $8 million per year now. However, despite this, they are still making some economic profit in the market, uncommon in the long run for a monopolistically competitive firm; the reason for this will be spoken about later on the menu.

Paired Side

Unemployment in Toronto

Unemployment in Toronto is a major current event issue for STK Steakhouse Toronto. As of April 2026, the unemployment rate for Toronto sits around 8.2%. This is observably larger than the national average of Canada at around 6.9%, an already concerning value. Having such a high unemployment rate is a major issue for a steakhouse like STK Toronto, leading to overall decreased salary statistics for the average Toronto citizen and a lack of sources of generated income for certain individuals.

Main Courses

Three essential economic concepts, prepared with care

I

First Course

Monopolistic Competition

STK Steakhouse is a firm in a monopolistically competitive market. Characteristics of monopolistic competition include a relatively large number of competitors and firms in the market, low to mid barriers to entry, and high levels of non-price competition. In the Toronto area, there are several different high-end steakhouses including Jacobs and Co., Blueblood, Black and Blue, STK, and more.

STK Steakhouse Toronto uses the method of non-price competition extremely well, significantly increasing their demand for the firm. They differentiate themselves from their competitors by advertising significantly on their Instagram, and especially by portraying a different vibe than your average high-end steakhouse, increasing their demand curve significantly. This makes them slightly different from the average monopolistically competitive firm where most firms in monopolistically competitive market structures earn 0 economic profit. However, due to these strategies of differentiation, proven establishment, and top-tier quality of the steakhouse, it allows them to earn positive economic profit despite being in the market for 10 years.

They are somewhat price makers and determine prices by looking at the performance of firms nearby — but it is essential to recognize that these firms have to be on their level in terms of quality.

This is extremely important and looking at a steakhouse like the Keg to determine their pricing strategy would not be effective. The reason for this is explored more in depth in the next main course, supply, demand, and market equilibrium.

II

Second Course

Supply, Demand & Market Equilibrium

Supply and demand significantly applies to STK Steakhouse Toronto. STK Toronto uses market equilibrium to determine their pricing methods by estimating their demand curve through methods of testing and looking at the performance of nearby firms that are on their level.

STK Toronto's supply is the products that they have on their menu; the profits made off these menu items are dependent on their demand curve, their pricing methods, and the specific profit margins made on each of these menu items. STK's steaks range from around $70–$250 with some specific outliers on the more expensive end with rare wagyu cuts. Most steaks sit around the $100 mark, which ties back to the reason why STK Steakhouse Toronto prices based on steakhouses of their caliber and level. STK Steakhouse could not use a steakhouse like The Keg's performance with their pricing to determine their own. This is because STK's beef and food quality is significantly higher than a steakhouse like The Keg, with a steak alone costing them about $40–$50. This shows a fairly strong profit margin of about 2–2.5 times the price. If they based their pricing off The Keg, their profit margins would be little to none, leading to economic losses when accounting for other external costs.

The steak at STK Steakhouse may generate them the most revenue, but it is not their item with the highest profit margins. Due to the luxury experience provided by STK Toronto with their amazing location, quality of food, and unique vibe, they can price discriminate and upcharge on certain items to rake in extra profit. Bottled water costs STK about $1 to get but they can sell it for $10–$15, giving an absolutely huge 10–15x profit margin.

III

Third Course

Price Elasticity of Demand

Price Elasticity of Demand is a concept that stems from the law of demand. The law of demand states that as price increases, consumers will buy less, and as price decreases, consumers will buy more. Price elasticity of demand refers to how much or how little consumers will buy in response to a price change.

The goal for a steakhouse is to make it as inelastic as possible to ensure that even if they have to increase the price, they can retain their customer base and generate revenue, not losing revenue due to a price increase. However, the reality is that the elasticity of the steakhouse depends heavily on the performance of the economy in the area. STK Steakhouse is considered a luxury good offering top-quality food, experience, and vibe. Due to the fact that Canada's economy is performing poorly right now with slowing GDP rates, increasing inflation rates, and major unemployment in Toronto, STK Steakhouse Toronto is more elastic currently.

Abid Chaudry owns several different restaurants across the globe and he stated that in Toronto specifically, he sees the most issue. He finds that the elasticity of demand for STK Steakhouse Toronto is significantly higher than luxury restaurants across the globe. An example that he gave was if you go to Alberta, luxury coffee shops have significant lines with luxury goods being significantly bought and used there due to the strength of the economy — showing more inelasticity where a slight price increase would retain most of their consumer base.

Another issue that makes STK Steakhouse Toronto specifically more elastic during economic downturns is from its consumer base that uses it as a place of business. When the economy is doing well, real estate agents make more deals and bring their customers to STK Toronto, allowing them to expand their business through referrals and accumulating more customers. This is a significant driver of STK Toronto's consumer base which occurs during economic growth and upturns — so when the market is slow, STK also loses this sector of their consumer base entirely.

Conclusion

In conclusion, STK Steakhouse Toronto is not just any regular steakhouse — it's a perfect example of a luxury firm feeling the economic downturn and recessions of Canada. In central Yorkville, STK Toronto encompasses the true feeling of luxury before you even walk through the door. It is surrounded by luxury and provides a unique experience the second you walk in. It's different — not being the same as your average monopolistically competitive firm, earning positive economic profit despite being in the market for 10 years. It differentiates itself, and is a perfect example of what non-price competition should really look like.

Despite this, STK Toronto is still experiencing loss compared to previous years of the business. Their revenue has fallen significantly from previously making $11 million to now only about $8 million per year. With GDP growth rates falling, inflation rates increasing, and the rapidly growing issue of unemployment in Toronto, the demand for STK has unfortunately fallen. This is not an issue on STK's part — it's an issue of the economy.

However, STK Toronto is not looking to leave the market. They continue to operate for the customers, and through their resilience they will overcome this economic downturn in Canada, and continue to remain the best, the most unique, and the most resilient steakhouse in Toronto. STK Steakhouse is owned by The One Group Hospitality Inc. — they are not new to this, owning 77 restaurants across the globe. They will overcome the economic downturns of the market. Even more reassuring for STK, it is in the hands and control of Abid Chaudry, an extremely experienced individual in the restaurant business owning several personally across the globe, and having a master's in economics — he understands how to handle this.

Many steakhouses are currently exiting the market, with 7,000 restaurants closing in Canada just last year. However, STK perseveres. When the economy returns back to its positive state, so will STK Toronto's demand, and it will show you why it's not just your average monopolistically competitive luxury steakhouse. It's different, and it will continue to provide its unique experience that differentiates itself, remaining at the top of the luxury steakhouse market.
